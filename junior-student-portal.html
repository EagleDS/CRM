FOUNDATIONAL
The building blocks of your combat game — 10 marks at every grading
Striking Techniques
▼
Your hands are your most important weapon. These five strikes must be clean, fast, and in the right order. The examiner is looking for correct technique — not power.
1
Jab
Lead hand straight punch. Fast and snappy — it sets everything else up. Shoulder forward, chin down, return immediately.
2
Cross
Rear hand straight punch. This is your power hand. Drive from the back foot, rotate the hip, and punch through the target.
3
Hook
A punch that comes around the side. Elbow up at shoulder height, pivot on the front foot. Aim for the jaw.
4
Rear Uppercut
Rear hand punching upward from below. Drop your shoulder slightly, drive up from the legs. Elbow points down at the start.
5
Lead Uppercut
Lead hand punching upward. Same movement as the rear uppercut but from the front hand. Keep your guard up with the rear hand.
Kicks
▼
Four kicks in the junior system. Every kick must show a proper chamber — knee up first — before the kick extends. This is what separates good technique from bad technique.
1
Rear Leg Roundhouse Kick
The most powerful kick. Chamber the rear knee up and across, pivot on the front foot, and drive the shin through the target. Hip rotates fully. Return to stance immediately.
2
Switch Kick
Switch your feet quickly so your rear leg becomes your front leg, then kick with the new rear leg. The switch must be fast and disguised — opponent shouldn't see it coming.
3
Front Kick
Chamber the knee up, then push straight forward with the heel or ball of the foot. This kick stops an opponent in their tracks. Keep your balance and return to stance.
4
Jumping Kick
A roundhouse kick performed from a jump. Chamber while in the air, extend at the peak of the jump, land balanced. The jump must be deliberate — not a hop.
Combinations
▼
Combinations are what make everything useful. Individual strikes are the vocabulary — combinations are the sentences. These build progressively through your belt levels.
White Belt → Yellow Belt
1 punch + kick
2 punches + kick
3 punches + kick
Orange Levels
1–4 punch combinations with kick integration
Punch → kick transitions
Kick → punch transitions
